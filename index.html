The Dead Internet Theory

Is the internet really run by bots?


The Dead Internet Theory claims that
most online activity is generated by
artificial intelligence. According
to this theory, much of what we see
on the internet, especially on social
media, is largely “fake” and created by
bots rather than real people.

Online discussions around the “Dead Internet Theory” often
reflect broader fears about artificial intelligence,
including misconceptions about about the extent that these
systems influence what we see online and that they may
eventually "take over".

What We Can Take Away

Stay Curious, Not Fearful

Technology feels mysterious when we stop asking how it works. The more we learn about AI and automation, the less they control us through fear. Curiosity keeps us informed and keeps the internet alive.

Practice Digital Literacy

  • Verify before you share
  • Follow diverse sources
  • Think before engaging
  • Stay informed about AI tools

Push for Digital Accountability

As technology becomes more powerful, laws and policies must evolve to protect users and ensure transparency. Supporting regulation around AI-generated content, data privacy, and online misinformation helps keep the digital space safe and fair for everyone.
